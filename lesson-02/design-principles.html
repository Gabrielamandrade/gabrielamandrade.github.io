Design Principle Document

Gabriela M. de Andrade

Alignment

Ted

ted.com

The TED site have made a good use of the alignment. Even the tags being scattered in a first view, it's still possible see that they follow a invisible line on center. The text, tags, and button have been centered making all the content, even being different, follow a standard. This help me to know where should I start to read, and what is the processes they ask me to do. The alignment makes me like of the layout, and even that it's not my plan make an account, unconsciously I would do.

Contrast

Ukecifras

ukecifras.com.br

I like the contrast that shows here. I can see the music of the week with more views as the biggest in the page and after the logo that calls my attention because of the choice of colors. Even the question above the search bar being helpful for keeping me there, I didnt like the contrast that have with the image behind. If I have not the intent of pay attention in all, I will not read what they have wrote.

Repetition

Amazon

amazon.com

Amazon repeat the same sequence to show their products. They created boxes using different colors, and similar colors to combine with the logo. It"s easy to navigate, but when you visit for the first time, you take much more time than you should, to decide what you want. Even with this boxes showing select products for you, they show too much options, that could be more filtered.
